Essential Elements

The key chemical elements that are crucial for maintaining health and supporting life functions.

Essential Elements for Life

Of the approximately 115 elements known, only the 19 highlighted in green in the Figure are absolutely required for humans

The 19 Essential Elements for Humans:

  1. Hydrogen: Combines with oxygen to form water, a crucial component of all bodily fluids.

  2. Carbon: Forms the backbone of organic molecules like proteins, carbohydrates, and fats.

  3. Nitrogen: Essential for building proteins and nucleic acids (DNA and RNA).

  4. Oxygen: Required for cellular respiration, which produces energy in cells.

  5. Sodium: Helps regulate fluid balance and nerve function.

  6. Magnesium: Supports muscle and nerve function, and is involved in over 300 biochemical reactions.

  7. Phosphorus: Important for energy production, bone health, and forming DNA and RNA.

  8. Sulfur: Key component of amino acids and proteins, and helps detoxify the body.

  9. Chlorine: Helps maintain fluid balance and is part of digestive juices like hydrochloric acid.

  10. Potassium: Regulates fluid balance, muscle contractions, and nerve signals.

  11. Calcium: Vital for building and maintaining strong bones and teeth, and for muscle and nerve function.

  12. Manganese: Supports metabolism, bone formation, and antioxidant defenses.

  13. Iron: Essential for oxygen transport in the blood and energy production.

  14. Cobalt: A component of vitamin B12, which is crucial for red blood cell formation and nervous system health.

  15. Copper: Helps with iron metabolism, the formation of red blood cells, and maintaining healthy nerves.

  16. Zinc: Important for immune function, wound healing, and DNA synthesis

  17. Selenium: Acts as an antioxidant and supports immune function.

  18. Molybdenum: Helps with the metabolism of certain amino acids and drugs.

  19. Iodine: Essential for producing thyroid hormones, which regulate metabolism.

Here are 10 Compounds also essential for humans:

  1. Water (H₂O): Contains Hydrogen and Oxygen and is crucial for hydration, digestion, temperature regulation, and biochemical reactions.

  2. Glucose (C₆H₁₂O₆): Composed of Carbon, Hydrogen, and Oxygen, it serves as a primary energy source for cells.

  3. Hemoglobin: Contains Carbon, Hydrogen, Oxygen, Nitrogen, and Iron, and is essential for oxygen transport in red blood cells.

  4. ATP (Adenosine Triphosphate): Includes Carbon, Hydrogen, Oxygen, Nitrogen, and Phosphorus, and provides energy for cellular processes.

  5. DNA (Deoxyribonucleic Acid): Made of Carbon, Hydrogen, Oxygen, Nitrogen, and Phosphorus, it stores genetic information for cellular function and reproduction.

  6. RNA (Ribonucleic Acid): Composed of Carbon, Hydrogen, Oxygen, Nitrogen, and Phosphorus, it translates genetic information from DNA into proteins.

  7. Cholesterol: Contains Carbon, Hydrogen, and Oxygen, and is important for cell membrane structure, hormone production, and vitamin D synthesis.

  8. Vitamins (e.g., Vitamin C): Made of Carbon, Hydrogen, and Oxygen, and are needed for various biochemical processes such as immune function and collagen synthesis.

  9. Sodium Chloride (NaCl): Consists of Sodium and Chlorine, and regulates fluid balance, nerve function, and muscle contractions.

  10. Calcium Phosphate (Ca₃(PO₄)₂): Includes Calcium, Phosphorus, and Oxygen, and is essential for bone and dental health.
